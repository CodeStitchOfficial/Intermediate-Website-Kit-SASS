Sleep easy; your site is secure.

Is your current website safe from hackers? Our websites are, but if you're using WordPress or a similar platform, it may not be.

Websites are either dynamic or static. Think of static websites like grab-and-go sandwiches. Everyone gets the same pre-made thing. Dynamic websites are like made-to-order sandwiches. Users can customize them, but they have to wait for it.

While the server makes the dynamic website, a hacker can slip in an extra ingredient (an SQL injection). The SQL injection tricks the database into serving malicious code, poisoning the sandwich.

Static websites don't have a database and come pre-assembled, so there's less for the hacker to attack. The sandwich is safe; it's already packaged. Bad actors can still tamper with it, but there's less risk.

Dynamic websites offer many advantages but aren't worth the risk if your needs are simple. Static websites offer more security and need less maintenance.

We use HTTPS and SSL/TLS certificates to encrypt data transmission. Adding a key layer of security to protect your website further.

Get discovered without buying ads.

Advertising can be effective, but it can get expensive. Search engine optimization, or SEO, gets you organic traffic without ads.

SEO can feel overwhelming, counterintuitive, mysterious, and slow, but it's also necessary.

There are two types of SEO: on-page and off-page. On-page SEO involves things on your website that you can control. For example, you need to:

  • add title tags and meta descriptions,
  • follow URL best practices,
  • use header tags,
  • structure content,
  • optimize images, and
  • ensure the website supports mobile devices.

We do the above to optimize your on-page SEO. We don't charge extra for this work; every web developer should do it by default.

Off-page SEO happens off your website. Backlinks, a link that goes to your website, are the big guns for off-page SEO. They build authority and improve your ranking in search results.

We don't offer off-page SEO services.
