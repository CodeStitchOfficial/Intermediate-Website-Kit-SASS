Mobile first and lightning fast

Too many small business websites look terrible on a phone. Usually this is because the website isn't responsive. Responsive websites adjust the layout at different screen sizes or breakpoints. This ensures it looks good and is easy to use no matter what type of device someone uses.

Most of your website's traffic will come from phones, then computers, and tablets in a distant third. Your website must look good and work well on all devices. But mobile is most important and needs prioritization.

Speed is another important factor. You'll lose visitors for each second a website takes to load. Studies show that 40% of people abandon a website if it takes 3 seconds to load. People rarely return after they leave website for slowness or usability issues.

Our approach addresses each of these priorities. We style for mobile first, and then add the adjustments for larger screens. We also check our design at various screen sizes throughout development. We don't stop adjusting it until it looks great at every breakpoint.

We hand-code every website using HTML, CSS, and a little JavaScript. This allows us to follow best practices and create streamlined, fast-loading websites. Every line of code serves a purpose. You won't need to worry about losing customers because your website took too long to load.

Sleep easy, your site is secure

Is your current website safe from hackers? Our websites are, but if you're using WordPress or a similar platform it may not be. These platforms are popular and easy to use. But that popularity also makes them popular targets. And the content management system or CMS that makes them easy to use creates a vulnerability.

Websites are either dynamic or static. The difference boils down to where the website builds and how it's served to the person. Think of static websites like a grab-and-go sandwich, it's already made so everyone gets the same thing. Dynamic websites are like a made-to-order sandwich. Each user can customize it but you have to wait for it to build.

What do sandwiches have to do with hackers? While the server makes the dynamic website, a hacker can slip in an extra ingredient (a SQL injection). The SQL injection tricks the database into serving malicious code. It poisons the sandwich. Static websites don't have a database and they're already assembled, so there's nothing for the hacker to attack. The sandwich is already wrapped and tamper-proof.

Dynamic websites offer many advantages, but if your needs are simple they aren't worth the risk. Static websites offer more security and need less maintenance. Platforms like WordPress need constant security updates, static websites don't. Using HTTPS and SSL/TLS certificates is enough for us to ensure your website is secure.

Get discovered without buying ads

Organic traffic costs you nothing. Advertising can be effective, but it gets expensive fast. Your goal should be to have people finding you on their own without you spending a penny. Search engine optimization or SEO is how you do that.

SEO can feel overwhelming. It involves technical terms and and requires a lot research and data analysis. It can be counter-intuitive---target low-volume search terms, not the popular high-volume ones. It can be mysterious---Google does what it wants when it wants and doesn't explain itself. It can be slow-going---SEO takes time, black-hat tricks may get you quick gains now, but you will be de-ranked later.

There are two types of SEO: on-page and off-page. On-page SEO involves things on your website that you can control. For example:

  • adding title tags and meta descriptions,
  • following URL best practices,
  • using header tags,
  • structuring content,
  • optimizing images, and
  • ensuring the website supports mobile devices.

We do everything we can to optimize your on-page SEO. We don't charge extra for this work, it's something every web developer should do by default. If they don't, that's a red flag.

Off-page SEO also contributes to your ranking, but it's done off your website. We're talking about backlinks here. A backlink is a link that links back to your website. They tell Google that your website is credible and provides quality content. Over time, this builds authority and improves your ranking in search results.

We don't offer off-page SEO services.
