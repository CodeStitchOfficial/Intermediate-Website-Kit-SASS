Your domain helps people find you; pick a good one.

Computers use numbers, but people need words. Every website has a numerical IP address. For example, no one would ever remember "208.80.153.224/27"—we use wikipedia.org. This word-based version is the domain. The Domain Name System (DNS) translates the readable domain into the IP address. Your domain is how people will remember your website, so pick a good one!

Every website needs an active domain (it expires if you forget to renew it). You must also maintain your DNS records to configure your website and email. Some common DNS records include:

  • A record: to assign your website's IP address to your domain;
  • CNAME record: to add an alias or alternate version of your domain;
  • MX record: to direct mail to your email server; and
  • TXT record: to prove you own the domain and that your emails aren't spam.

There are many more types of DNS records. We can manage your domain for $20 a year if this stuff gives you a headache. Should you cancel our service, we'll return your domain without hassle. You always own your domain, even if we buy it for you.

Hosting connects your website with the world.

Web hosting connects your website to the Internet. Your website's files live on the host's server. When you visit a website, your computer pings the host's server, which returns the files to you.

A content delivery network, or CDN, works with the web host to improve website performance. A CDN is a global network of servers that can serve a cached version of your website. This network can make the website faster and more secure. Sharing the load also reduces the likelihood of crashing.

Our web host offers a CDN at no extra charge. We include hosting with our Subscription websites. If you buy a Standard website, you can add hosting for $25 a month.

Clear copy converts

Don't make your customers think. Your website should be effortless to read and persuasive. Storytelling accomplishes this.

Your website and brand need to tell the story of a hero. In this story, the hero overcomes obstacles, solves their problems, and transforms themself. Your business is not the hero; your customer is. Your business is the mentor guiding them to success.

This narrative structure puts your customer first. Customers don't care about you; they care about how you can solve their problems. This approach shows how your product or service will improve their lives. Features and benefits are essential, but they aren't convincing. You need to show them how your offer will improve their lives.

Explicit stories engage your customers and persuade them to act. Don't settle for bland copy that lists boring facts and fails to inspire action. You have a great offer; present it in a way that lets it shine.

We've applied this approach throughout our website. If you have us write your copy, we can do the same. We'll use the same framework, keyword research, and strong calls to action.
